PhD Student · Agricultural & Applied Economics · Virginia Tech

I am a first-year PhD student at Virginia Tech, advised by Dr. Le Wang. My research interests are in applied econometrics, health economics, and causal inference. My dissertation examines the causal effect of broadband expansion on county-level mental health outcomes using staggered difference-in-differences and causal machine learning methods. I also contribute to the Data Science for the Public Good program at Virginia Tech.

Research

Broadband Expansion and Mental Health Outcomes: Causal Evidence from U.S. Counties
Dissertation Chapter · Work in Progress

Examines the causal effect of broadband infrastructure rollout on county-level mental health using staggered difference-in-differences and FCC Form 477 deployment data. Applies causal forest methods to identify heterogeneous treatment effects and characterize which county subpopulations benefit most from broadband access.

The Impact of Campaign Funds for Childcare Policies on Female Representation
Submitted · Gender and Politics

Uses two-way fixed effects and staggered difference-in-differences (Callaway–Sant’Anna) to estimate the causal effect of Campaign Funds for Childcare (CFCC) policies on women’s candidacy across federal, state, and local elections. Finds heterogeneous effects with local elections showing meaningful increases in female participation.

Economic Efficiency of Mineral Oil–Insecticide Combinations in PVY Management
Manuscript · Work in Progress

Evaluates six mineral oil and insecticide spray programs using multi-year field trial data from Idaho and a partial budgeting framework. Finds the at-plant insecticide plus weekly oil treatment generates the highest net revenue across potato size classes.
